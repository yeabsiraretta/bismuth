# Johnny.Decimal System

The **Johnny.Decimal system** is a method for organizing files and information using a hierarchical decimal notation.

## Structure

### Areas (10-19, 20-29, etc.)

Top-level categories, max 10 per vault.

**Example**:

- `10-19` Personal
- `20-29` Projects
- `30-39` Work
- `40-49` Archive

### Categories (11, 12, 13, etc.)

Subcategories within areas, max 10 per area.

**Example** (within 10-19 Personal):

- `11` Notes
- `12` Journal
- `13` Ideas
- `14` Reading

### IDs (11.01, 11.02, etc.)

Individual items, max 99 per category.

**Example** (within 11 Notes):

- `11.01` Welcome to Bismuth
- `11.02` Johnny.Decimal System
- `11.03` Zettelkasten Method

## Benefits

1. **Limited Choices** - Max 10 folders at each level reduces decision fatigue
2. **Clear Navigation** - Decimal notation provides obvious paths
3. **Scalable** - System grows with your knowledge
4. **Memorable** - Numbers are easier to remember than long paths

### Why does Johnny.Decimal limit to 10 areas? #card #organization #pkm

The constraint forces deliberate categorization — with only 10 top-level areas, you must think carefully about structure. This prevents sprawl and ensures every note has a clear home.
