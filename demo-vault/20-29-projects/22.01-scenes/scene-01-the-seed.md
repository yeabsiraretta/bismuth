# Scene 1: The Seed

Mara had always kept her notes in neat rows. Lined paper, color-coded tabs, every idea in its designated slot. The library's card catalog was her inspiration - everything in its place, and a place for everything.

But today, something was different.

She opened her morning journal and found a vine drawn across the margin. Not by her hand - the ink was a shade of green she didn't own. It traced from yesterday's note about photosynthesis to a scribbled thought about neural networks three pages back.

"That's... odd," she muttered, flipping between the pages. The vine was real ink on real paper, connecting two ideas she had never consciously linked.

She pulled out her reference notebook - the one she used for her [[11.03-zettelkasten-method|Zettelkasten]]-style index cards. Card #247 (photosynthesis) and Card #89 (neural networks) were stacked on her desk, though she was certain she'd filed them separately.

Between them lay a new card she hadn't written. Card #247.89, it read at the top. Below, in handwriting that was hers but somehow sharper:

> "Both systems transform raw input (light/data) into structured growth through layered networks. The parallel is not metaphorical - it is architectural."

Mara stared at the card. The knowledge was growing.

---

She spent the afternoon testing it. She placed her notes on quantum entanglement next to her recipe for sourdough starter. Nothing happened for hours. Then, just as she was about to give up, a tendril of blue ink appeared, curling from the word "entanglement" toward "culture."

A new card materialized: "Both depend on maintaining connection across distance and time. The starter carries information from previous generations; entangled particles carry state across space."

> [!warning] Draft Note
> This scene needs more sensory detail. The library should feel alive - creaking shelves, dust motes, the smell of old paper.
